# Data Privacy Reflection

## Research notes

Focus Bear handles user data and company information, so privacy and confidentiality are important. Based on the privacy policy, user data may be collected when people use the website or app, contact the company, or interact with Focus Bear services. This means the company needs to handle personal information carefully and only use it for proper business purposes.

Confidential data at Focus Bear could include user information, account details, internal documents, security findings, product plans, source code, business information, credentials, access details, and anything shared internally that is not meant to be public.

Some good practices for handling confidential data are:

- only access information needed for the task
- avoid downloading or copying sensitive data unless required
- keep work inside approved company systems
- do not share internal information in public channels
- use strong passwords and MFA where available
- be careful when using AI tools or external services
- check who can access a file before sharing it
- report anything suspicious or accidental as soon as possible

If I suspect a data breach or accidental disclosure, I should not try to hide it or fix it quietly. I should report it to the right person as soon as possible, explain what happened, what data may be involved, who may have accessed it, and what steps I have already taken. Quick reporting matters because the team needs time to assess the risk and respond properly.

## Reflection

In my daily tasks, I can handle data securely by being careful with what I access, where I store information, and who I share it with. I should also avoid putting company information, user data, code, credentials, or security findings into external tools unless I have approval.

Sensitive information should be stored only in approved systems. If I need to share something, I should check the permissions first and only share it with people who need access. If information is no longer needed, I should follow the proper process instead of keeping extra copies on my own device.

Some common mistakes that can lead to privacy issues include sending information to the wrong person, leaving files with open permissions, copying data into personal notes, using weak passwords, uploading sensitive content into AI tools, or discussing internal information in the wrong place. These can be avoided by slowing down, checking permissions, using approved tools, and asking if I am unsure.

## Habit I will adopt

One habit I will adopt is to pause before sharing or storing any internal information and ask myself:

- Does this need to be shared?
- Who actually needs access?
- Is this the approved place to store it?
- Could this contain user data or confidential company information?

This simple check should help reduce mistakes.

## Key learning

My main takeaway is that data privacy is not only about avoiding major breaches. It is also about small daily habits, like using the right tools, checking access, not oversharing, and reporting mistakes early.

As a Cyber Security Analyst Intern, I need to treat company and user information carefully, especially because Focus Bear users may trust the product as part of their daily routines and wellbeing.
