# Competitive Landscape

## Research notes

I looked at Focus Bear and compared it with a few other productivity/focus apps: Freedom, Cold Turkey, Tiimo, and Routinery.

Freedom and Cold Turkey seem stronger if the main goal is simply blocking distracting websites or apps. Cold Turkey especially looks strict and harder to bypass, which would be useful for someone who wants strong blocking without too many extra features.

Tiimo seems more focused on visual planning and supporting people with executive function challenges. Routinery is more focused on building routines and habits in a simple step-by-step way.

Focus Bear seems to sit somewhere between these tools. It is not just a blocker, and it is not just a routine app. It combines routines, focus sessions, distraction blocking, breaks, and support for people who struggle with focus and consistency.

## What makes Focus Bear different?

The main difference I noticed is that Focus Bear seems more complete as a daily support tool. It is trying to help with the full flow of the day, not just one part of it.

For example, Freedom and Cold Turkey are useful for blocking distractions, but they do not seem as focused on routines, breaks, and guiding the user through the day. Tiimo and Routinery help more with planning and routines, but Focus Bear also adds stricter focus support and distraction blocking.

Another difference is that Focus Bear seems clearly built around ADHD, autism, and executive function challenges. That makes the product feel more specific instead of being a general productivity app for everyone.

## Why would I choose Focus Bear over competitors?

If I were a user, I would choose Focus Bear if I wanted one tool that helps me start the day properly, stay focused during work, block distractions, and remember to take breaks.

I think it would be especially useful for someone who already knows what they need to do, but struggles with actually starting or staying consistent. The app seems more hands-on than a normal habit tracker or to-do list.

I also like that it supports breaks and routines, because productivity is not just about forcing focus for long periods. If the app helps prevent burnout as well, that makes it more useful long-term.

## One feature other apps have that Focus Bear may not have

One thing Tiimo seems to do well is visual planning. It presents the day in a very clear visual way, which could be helpful for people who like seeing their schedule laid out simply.

Focus Bear has strong focus and routine features, but from what I saw, competitors like Tiimo may feel more polished on the planning side. That is one area where users might prefer another app if they mainly want a visual daily planner.

## One improvement Focus Bear could make

One improvement Focus Bear could make is to make the planning experience more visual and easier to understand at a glance.

For example, a simple daily timeline or overview could help users see their routines, focus sessions, breaks, and tasks in one place. This could make the app feel easier to use, especially for people who get overwhelmed when information is spread across different sections.

I do think Focus Bear’s strength is that it combines routines, focus, and blocking. Improving the visual planning side could make that experience even clearer for users.
