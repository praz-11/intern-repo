# OHS Laptop Ergonomics

## Research notes

Working directly from a laptop for long periods can cause a few issues. The screen is usually too low, so I end up looking down, which can lead to neck and shoulder strain. The keyboard and trackpad are also close together, so it is easy to sit in a cramped position or put strain on the wrists.

A better setup would be to raise the laptop with a stand or even a stable stack of books, and use an external keyboard and mouse. That way, the screen can sit closer to eye level while my arms and wrists stay in a more comfortable position.

For the desk setup, the main things I need to watch are screen height, chair position, and how long I sit without moving. Ideally, my screen should be around eye level, my shoulders should stay relaxed, and my feet should be flat on the floor. I should also avoid sitting in the same position for too long.

Some useful daily habits would be taking short movement breaks, checking my posture during the day, and looking away from the screen regularly to reduce eye strain.

## Reflection

One change I can make is to raise my laptop so I am not constantly looking down at the screen. I can also use an external keyboard and mouse when I am working for longer periods.

A behaviour change I want to make is to take breaks before I start feeling uncomfortable, rather than waiting until my neck or shoulders already feel stiff. I can also do quick posture checks during the day.

I can use Focus Bear reminders to schedule movement breaks and posture checks. This would help because I often get focused on the task and forget to move around.

## Change I made

I adjusted my setup so my laptop screen is higher and easier to look at. I also plan to use an external keyboard and mouse when possible, especially for longer work sessions.

## What I noticed

Even a small change to the screen height made the setup feel more comfortable. I also noticed that taking short breaks helps me reset my posture and focus better when I come back to the task.
