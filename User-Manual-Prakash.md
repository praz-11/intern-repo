# User Manual for Prakash

## My role

- I am working as a Cyber Security Analyst Intern.
- I will mainly be supporting security governance, ISO 27001/compliance-related work, documentation, and any security tasks assigned during the internship.
- The days and times I work are Wednesday and Thursday/Friday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM.
- I am based in Melbourne and will work on a remote configuration. 

## How I Work Best

- I do my best work when the task is clear and I understand the expected outcome.
- I like to understand the reason behind a task, not just the task itself. This helps me do the work properly instead of just completing it quickly.
- I am comfortable working independently, but I appreciate check-ins early on so I know I am on the right track.

## Communication Style

- I prefer messages to be clear and direct.
- Written communication works well for me, especially for tasks, feedback, and instructions.
- The best way to reach me is through the agreed team communication channel, such as Discord, email, or GitHub issues, depending on the task.
- If something is urgent, it helps if that is clearly mentioned.
- If you need something from me, a clear deadline or priority level helps me plan my work properly.

## Collaboration & Teamwork

- I work best in a team when expectations are clear and people communicate early if something changes.
- I am happy to ask questions when needed, but I usually try to understand the task first before asking.
- My biggest pet peeve in teamwork is unclear communication where people assume everyone understands the same context.
- If I seem quiet in a meeting, it usually means I am listening, taking things in, or trying to understand the context before speaking.
- I prefer to contribute thoughtfully rather than speak just for the sake of saying something.

## Focus & Productivity

- I stay focused by breaking tasks into smaller steps and working through them one at a time.
- I like using simple notes or checklists to track what I have done, what is pending, and what I need to ask.
- Things that distract me include unclear tasks, too many context switches, or not knowing what the priority is.
- The best way to support my productivity is to give clear priorities, expected outcomes, and feedback when something needs to be improved.
- I also work better when I can focus on one task properly instead of switching between too many things at once.

## Feedback & Growth

- I prefer feedback to be direct, specific, and focused on what I can improve.
- I do not mind constructive feedback. I would rather know early if something needs to be changed than continue in the wrong direction.
- When I make a mistake, the best way to let me know is to explain what was wrong, what the expected approach was, and how I can avoid it next time.
- I appreciate examples, especially for documentation or security governance tasks where the expected format matters.
- I am still early in my cybersecurity career, so I value feedback that helps me improve both technically and professionally.

## Fun Facts & Quirks

- One thing people may misunderstand about me is that I can seem quiet at first, but I am usually just trying to understand the environment and the people around me.
- A fun fact about me is that I enjoy dance and football, and I have been involved in both for a long time.
- My work superpower is being patient and methodical. I like working through details carefully and making sure I understand what is actually happening before jumping to conclusions.
