# Professionalism Reflection

## Research notes

Professional behaviour in a casual work environment does not mean being overly formal all the time. To me, it means being respectful, reliable, clear in communication, and taking responsibility for the work I do.

Examples of professional behaviour include:
- replying to messages in a reasonable time
- being clear if I am blocked or need help
- attending meetings prepared
- respecting other people’s time
- giving updates without needing to be chased
- treating feedback as something to learn from
- being friendly while still being accountable

Examples of unprofessional behaviour would be:
- ignoring messages or tasks
- missing meetings without notice
- being rude or dismissive
- blaming others instead of taking responsibility
- making jokes that could make someone uncomfortable
- not following through on agreed work
- hiding mistakes instead of raising them early

For remote teams, respectful communication is especially important because tone can be misunderstood in text. I should keep messages clear, polite, and direct. If there is a disagreement, it is better to focus on the issue and the next step rather than making it personal.

Giving and receiving feedback professionally means focusing on actions and outcomes, not attacking the person. If I receive feedback, I should listen properly, ask questions if I need clarification, and apply it to improve my work.

## Reflection

I have seen good teamwork in previous work environments where people communicated clearly, helped each other when things got busy, and escalated issues early instead of letting them become bigger problems. What made it effective was that people were reliable and did not make others guess what was happening.

To make sure my communication is clear and respectful, I can:
- keep updates short and specific
- ask focused questions when I am stuck
- explain what I have already tried
- avoid vague messages like “this does not work”
- be mindful of tone, especially in written messages
- assume good intent when receiving feedback or clarification

I can help create a positive work environment by being approachable, reliable, and respectful. I do not need to be overly serious all the time, but I should still make sure my work is done properly and my communication is professional.

## Preparing for meetings

Before meetings with supervisors or team members, I can prepare by:
- checking what the meeting is about
- reviewing my current tasks
- writing down any blockers or questions
- being ready to give a short progress update
- noting any actions I need to take afterwards

This would help me use meeting time properly and avoid forgetting important points.

## Being proactive during the internship

Being proactive during the internship means not waiting to be told every small step. If I finish a task, I should check what needs to happen next, update the issue, or ask for the next priority.

It also means raising blockers early. If I am unsure about something, I should try to understand it first, but not stay stuck silently for too long.

## Following up and escalation

If I need information or action from someone, I would first send a clear message explaining what I need and why. If I do not get a response, I would follow up politely after a reasonable amount of time.

If the issue is blocking my work or has security, privacy, or professional conduct impact, I would escalate it sooner. I would include the context, what I have tried, who I contacted, and why it needs attention.

For serious issues like major breaches of professional conduct, privacy concerns, or security incidents, I would follow the correct internal process and contact the right person quickly rather than trying to handle it quietly.

## Things I will not do

Some things I will not do during the internship:
- ignore tasks or messages without explanation
- share confidential information outside approved channels
- use company information in external tools without approval
- make changes to systems without permission
- speak disrespectfully to teammates or users
- hide mistakes or delays
- pretend I understand something if I need clarification
